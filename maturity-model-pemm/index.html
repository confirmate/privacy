Privacy engineering maturity model

PEMM — Align privacy work with structured maturity levels

This interactive reference summarizes the Privacy Engineering Maturity Model (PEMM). Each activity describes how privacy work evolves from ad-hoc practices (Level 1) to repeatable and automated capabilities (Level 3). Use this page to understand expectations, discuss roadmaps, or review coverage of your Secure Development Lifecycle (SDL).

How to read this model

Every activity contains three maturity levels. The narrative explains the intent for that level, while the quality criteria list objective signals that the threshold has been met. Start by identifying your current practices, then use the criteria to define concrete next steps for advancing privacy engineering across the SDL.

Level 1 — Emerging
Privacy is addressed manually and typically in response to the most critical risks.
Level 2 — Managed
Processes become systematic and benefit from dedicated roles, checklists, and semi-automated tooling.
Level 3 — Integrated
Privacy is embedded in day-to-day delivery, with automation, continuous monitoring, and proactive improvements.
